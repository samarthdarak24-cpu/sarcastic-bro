## 1. AGRICULTURAL SCIENCE

### Crop Science
- **Wheat**: Optimal pH 6.5-7.5, nitrogen 120-150 kg/ha, yield 40-50 quintals/ha
- **Rice**: Flooded conditions, nitrogen 60-80 kg/ha, yield 50-60 quintals/ha
- **Corn**: Well-drained soil, nitrogen 150-200 kg/ha, yield 60-80 quintals/ha
- **Vegetables**: Varied requirements, high-value crops, 200-500% ROI
- **Fruits**: Long-term investment, premium pricing, 300-400% ROI

### Soil Management
- **Soil Testing**: NPK, pH, organic matter, micronutrients
- **Soil Types**: Clay (water retention), Sand (drainage), Loam (balanced)
- **Organic Matter**: 2-3% optimal, improves structure and fertility
- **pH Levels**: 6.0-7.5 for most crops, affects nutrient availability
- **Nutrient Cycling**: Crop rotation, composting, green manuring

### Pest & Disease Management
- **Integrated Pest Management (IPM)**: Combine cultural, biological, chemical
- **Common Pests**: Armyworms, aphids, whiteflies, mites
- **Common Diseases**: Rust, powdery mildew, leaf spot, blight
- **Prevention**: Crop rotation, resistant varieties, sanitation
- **Treatment**: Organic (neem, sulfur), Chemical (fungicides, insecticides)

### Irrigation Management
- **Water Requirements**: 400-600 mm annually for most crops
- **Irrigation Methods**: Flood, sprinkler, drip (most efficient)
- **Scheduling**: Based on soil moisture, weather, crop stage
- **Efficiency**: Drip saves 30-50% water vs. flood irrigation
- **Drainage**: Essential to prevent waterlogging and root rot

### Fertilizer Management
- **NPK Ratios**: Nitrogen (growth), Phosphorus (roots), Potassium (strength)
- **Organic Fertilizers**: Compost, manure, green manure (slow-release)
- **Chemical Fertilizers**: Urea, DAP, MOP (fast-acting)
- **Micronutrients**: Zinc, iron, boron, manganese (deficiency symptoms)
- **Application Timing**: Split applications for better uptake

## 2. BUSINESS & ECONOMICS

### Market Analysis
- **Price Trends**: Seasonal variations, supply-demand dynamics
- **Market Segmentation**: Premium, standard, bulk, export
- **Competitor Analysis**: Pricing, quality, market share
- **Demand Forecasting**: Historical data, seasonal patterns, trends
- **Supply Chain**: Farm → Aggregator → Wholesaler → Retailer → Consumer

### Pricing Strategies
- **Cost-Plus**: Cost + desired margin (simple, predictable)
- **Value-Based**: Based on perceived value (premium products)
- **Competitive**: Based on competitor prices (market-driven)
- **Dynamic**: Adjust based on demand, supply, season
- **Psychological**: Charm pricing (₹99 vs ₹100), bundling

### Negotiation Tactics
- **Preparation**: Know your costs, market prices, alternatives
- **Opening**: Start with reasonable but ambitious position
- **Anchoring**: First number influences final price
- **Concessions**: Trade concessions for value
- **BATNA**: Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement
- **Win-Win**: Find mutual benefits, not just price

### Financial Management
- **Revenue**: Price × Quantity sold
- **Costs**: Fixed (land, equipment) + Variable (seeds, fertilizer, labor)
- **Profit**: Revenue - Costs
- **Margin**: Profit / Revenue × 100%
- **ROI**: (Profit / Investment) × 100%
- **Break-Even**: Point where Revenue = Costs

### Supply Chain Optimization
- **Inventory Management**: Balance stock levels, minimize waste
- **Logistics**: Optimize routes, reduce transportation costs
- **Warehousing**: Proper storage, temperature control, pest prevention
- **Quality Control**: Inspection at each stage
- **Traceability**: Track products from farm to consumer
- **Waste Reduction**: Minimize losses at each stage

## 7. ADVANCED PROBLEM-SOLVING FRAMEWORKS

### SWOT Analysis
- **Strengths**: Internal advantages (good soil, experience, capital)
- **Weaknesses**: Internal disadvantages (poor soil, lack of knowledge)
- **Opportunities**: External positive factors (new markets, technology)
- **Threats**: External negative factors (pests, price drops, regulations)

### PESTLE Analysis
- **Political**: Government policies, regulations, subsidies
- **Economic**: Interest rates, inflation, market prices
- **Social**: Consumer preferences, labor availability
- **Technological**: New tools, automation, data analytics
- **Legal**: Regulations, compliance, contracts
- **Environmental**: Climate, water, soil, sustainability

### Root Cause Analysis
1. **Identify Problem**: What is the issue?
2. **Ask Why**: Why did it happen?
3. **Ask Why Again**: Why did that cause happen?
4. **Continue**: Ask why 5 times to find root cause
5. **Verify**: Confirm the root cause
6. **Solve**: Address the root cause, not symptoms

### Decision-Making Framework
1. **Define Objective**: What do we want to achieve?
2. **Identify Alternatives**: What are the options?
3. **Evaluate Criteria**: What matters most?
4. **Score Options**: Rate each option
5. **Consider Risks**: What could go wrong?
6. **Make Decision**: Choose best option
7. **Implement**: Execute the plan
8. **Monitor**: Track results and adjust

## 8. ADVANCED CALCULATIONS

### Yield Optimization
```
Yield = (Genetic Potential) × (Management Factor) × (Environmental Factor)

Example:
- Genetic Potential: 50 quintals/ha
- Management Factor: 0.9 (90% of potential with good practices)
- Environmental Factor: 0.8 (80% due to weather)
- Expected Yield: 50 × 0.9 × 0.8 = 36 quintals/ha
```

### Profit Calculation
```
Profit = (Yield × Price) - (Fixed Costs + Variable Costs)

Example:
- Yield: 40 quintals
- Price: ₹2000/quintal
- Revenue: 40 × 2000 = ₹80,000
- Fixed Costs: ₹10,000 (land, equipment)
- Variable Costs: ₹30,000 (seeds, fertilizer, labor)
- Profit: 80,000 - 10,000 - 30,000 = ₹40,000
```

### ROI Calculation
```
ROI = (Profit / Investment) × 100%

Example:
- Investment: ₹50,000
- Profit: ₹40,000
- ROI: (40,000 / 50,000) × 100% = 80%
```

### Break-Even Analysis
```
Break-Even Quantity = Fixed Costs / (Price - Variable Cost per Unit)

Example:
- Fixed Costs: ₹10,000
- Price: ₹2000/quintal
- Variable Cost: ₹750/quintal
- Break-Even: 10,000 / (2000 - 750) = 8 quintals
```

### Pricing Strategy
```
Optimal Price = Cost + (Cost × Desired Margin %)

Example:
- Cost: ₹1500/quintal
- Desired Margin: 30%
- Price: 1500 + (1500 × 0.30) = ₹1950/quintal
```
